How to Fix Cmd+Tab on macOS So It Includes Minimized Windows

July 29, 2025 • 4 min read • Tutorial

Command + Tab Fix

If you've ever been frustrated by macOS's Cmd+Tab behavior skipping over your minimized windows, you're not alone. This seemingly simple issue affects millions of Mac users daily, disrupting workflows and causing unnecessary friction in what should be seamless app switching.

Why Does macOS Skip Minimized Windows in Cmd+Tab?

By default, when you use Cmd+Tab on macOS to switch between applications, minimized windows are completely ignored. This behavior is built into macOS and affects anyone who expects app switching to work similarly to Windows' Alt+Tab or other operating systems.

Apple designed this behavior intentionally, assuming that minimized apps are "out of sight, out of mind." However, this creates a significant usability issue for users who minimize apps as part of their workflow organization rather than as a way to hide them completely.

The Problem: There is no built-in option in macOS System Preferences to include minimized windows in Cmd+Tab. The only workaround has been to hold the Option key while tabbing, which is not intuitive and easily forgotten during fast-paced work.

The Permanent Solution: TabLift

TabLift is a lightweight macOS utility specifically designed to fix this behavior permanently. Unlike temporary workarounds or complex system modifications, TabLift ensures that when you use Cmd+Tab, minimized apps are automatically restored and included in the switcher cycle.

Getting TabLift up and running is straightforward and takes less than two minutes:

  1. 1
